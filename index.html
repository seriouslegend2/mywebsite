About me

I am a Computer Science undergraduate at IIIT Sri City, specializing in full-stack development, distributed computing, and optimization techniques. My work primarily revolves around creating scalable, efficient systems by leveraging fog computing and edge computing architectures. I integrate advanced algorithms, such as deep reinforcement learning (DRL) and Pareto optimization, to address complex challenges in task scheduling, resource allocation, and computational efficiency.

In my projects, I focus on real-time data processing, particularly EEG signal analysis for brain-computer interfaces (BCIs) and healthcare systems. I aim to optimize latency, energy consumption, and resource utilization while ensuring seamless interaction between humans and machines. I believe that as computational power at the edge and fog computing nodes increases, it will unlock new possibilities for machine learning and real-time applications, especially in healthcare and BCIs, enabling innovative, user-centric solutions that can significantly enhance human well-being.

Portfolio

  • Fog-Based Video Processing with Pareto and ACO

    This project demonstrates a fog computing-based system for detecting the presence of the red color in a video stream. By leveraging Docker containers, OpenCV for image processing, Pareto optimization for fog node selection, and Ant Colony Optimization (ACO) for task offloading, this project ensures efficient task offloading and processing across a distributed fog network.

  • Hunger Hearts

    Every day, a significant amount of food goes to waste in restaurants, canteens, institutes and organizations, while many in slums suffer from hunger and malnutrition. Our project aims to bridge this gap by connecting food donors (restaurants, canteens, and organizations) with slums in need. Through a user-managed system, delivery boys are assigned to collect surplus food from donors and distribute it to slum areas efficiently. A platform that coordinates surplus food donations and facilitates distribution through a network of delivery personnel

  • Heart Attack Analysis and Prediction

    This project is focused on the analysis and prediction of heart attack risks based on medical attributes such as age, blood pressure, cholesterol, maximum heart rate, and others. Using machine learning techniques, the goal is to predict whether a patient is likely to suffer from a heart attack, which is a critical health problem worldwide.

  • 2DP-FHS: 2D Pareto Optimized Fog Head Selection for Multiple EEG Healthcare Data Analysis and Computations

    This project addresses the limitations of cloud-based EEG systems by integrating fog computing for real-time analysis and distributed computation. Using Pareto optimization, an optimal Fog Head (FH) is selected in a two-layer architecture connecting multiple EEG devices to fog nodes. Delay analysis demonstrates the system's scalability and efficiency with varying device count
